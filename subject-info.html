Subject Information

AP Physics 1

  • Kinematics: Describing motion using velocity, acceleration, displacement, and time.
  • Dynamics: Newton’s Laws and how forces affect motion.
  • Work & Energy: Work-energy theorem, kinetic and potential energy, conservation laws.
  • Momentum:Impulse-momentum theorem, collisions, and conservation of momentum.
  • Rotational Motion: Torque, rotational inertia, and rotational kinematics.
  • Energy and Momentum of Rotating Systems: Rotational energy, angular momentum, angular impulse
  • Oscillations: Springs, pendulums, simple harmonic motion
  • Fluids: Pressure, conservation laws, Bernoulli

AP Precalculus

  • Polynomial and Rational Functions: Rates of Change, rational functions, transformations
  • Exponential and Logarithmic Functions: Exponential, Logarithmic, Inverse functions
  • Trigonometric and Polar Functions: Unit Circle, Trigonmetry, inverse trigonometric functions, polar graphs

Chemistry Topics

  • Atomic Structure and Properties: Basics of atoms, subatomic particles, and atomic properties.
  • Molecular and Ionic Compound Structure and Properties: How molecules and ionic compounds form and their characteristics.
  • Intermolecular Forces and Properties: Forces between molecules and how they affect physical properties.
  • Chemical Reactions: Types of reactions, balancing equations, and reaction mechanisms.
  • Kinetics: Reaction rates, factors affecting rates, and rate laws.
  • Thermodynamics: Energy changes in reactions, enthalpy, and entropy.
  • Equilibrium: Dynamic balance in chemical reactions, Le Chatelier’s Principle.
  • Acids and Bases: Properties, theories, pH, and titrations.
  • Applications of Thermodynamics: Real-world uses like engines, refrigerators, and phase changes.
